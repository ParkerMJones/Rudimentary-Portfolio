Interstice

Sudaunt

Electronic

Suture Sonics

A collection of tumbling improvisations that leave you feeling like you're in the dryer.

"Interstice" is Sudaunt's foray into the land of dissolved textures and semi-ambient anti-rhythms. Sequential structures are ditched in favor of raw exploration, and the result is a dreamy compilation of recorded spontaneity. The moods differ here: the gristly garble of "Club Song" suggests the stomach rumblings of a queasy robot, while "Redball" takes one on a reverential trip through subjective nostalgia. In "Underwater Cereal", bubbles rise to the surface overhead while the listener dives in novel waters; "Rainforest" slithers through the wet grounds of the Amazon in 7 minutes of meandering meditation. Simplicity here is key: never overcomplicated, this album does well to accompany a half-busy mind.
